The Rest of the 272 Timeline


The history of the actions of the Georgetown community following the 1838 sale of 272 enslaved people by Georgetown University and the Jesuits of the Maryland Province of the Society of Jesus, displayed chronologically.

June-November 1838

The 1838 Sale

In 1838, Georgetown University and the Jesuits of the Maryland Province of the Society of Jesus sell 272 enslaved people to various slave holders and plantations farther south.

Learn More
November 27, 1848

The Van de Velde Letters

Ten years after the sale, Jesuit priest James Van de Velde sends a pair of letters to Georgetown University, pleading for the University to fulfill their promise to support the religious instruction of the 272 enslaved people sold by Georgetown by contributing to the construction of a church in Louisiana. Both letters are ignored by the University.

1838-1989

161 Years Pass

Georgetown University and the Maryland Jesuits do nothing to acknowledge their actions for over 150 years.

October 1, 1989

Black Georgetown Remembered

As part of its bicentennial celebration in 1988-89, Georgetown University honors its African American Community with this video. Through the recollections of the members of the Georgetown black community, the documentary pays tribute to a community that thrived amidst many injustices. It features the remembrances of the decades of the 1940s to the 1950s when the population of Georgetown changed markedly.

September 24, 2015

The Jesuit Plantation Project

History Professor Sharon M. Leon, Ph.D, creates the Jesuit Plantation Project. This project is focused on uncovering details about the lives and experiences of the enslaved involved in the 1838 sale, rather than on their Jesuit owners. This is the first step towards finding the descendants of the original 272.

Check it out
September 24, 2015

Georgetown Working Group on Slavery, Memory, and Reconciliation

President DeGioia announces the Working Group on Slavery, Memory, and Reconciliation led by Fr. David Collins, S.J., Ph.D. This group is tasked with providing advice and recommendations to the president on "how to best acknowledge and recognize Georgetown's historical relationship with the institution of slavery."

Learn More
November 13, 2015

Renaming Mulledy Hall and McSherry Hall

President DeGioia announces he will be accepting a recommendation from the recently created Working Group to rename Mulley and McSherry Hall. The new names of these buildings would be Freedom Hall and Remeberance Hall, respectively.

Learn More

April 16, 2016

The New York Times writes about the 272

Rachel L. Swarns from The New York Times writes an article titled "272 Slaves Were Sold to Save Georgetown. What Does It Owe Their Descendants?" that details the sale of 1838 and the steps Georgetown has taken and is planning to take in an attempt to make amends.

2016

Slavery, History, Memory and Reconciliation Project

The Jesuits create the Slavery, History, Memory, and Reconciliation Project to learn from descendants, research the expeirences of the people held in slavery by the Jesuits, and the communicate their history to the general public.

Check it out
2016

GU272 Descendants Association

The descendants of the GU272 create an association that aspires to represent the greater common good of all descendants of Jesuit and Catholic slaveholding in the United States.

Check it out
April 18, 2017

Liturgy of Rememberance

Georgetown and the Society of Jesus' Maryland Province officially apologize for their roles in the 1838 sale in front of more than 100 descendants.

Watch Here
April 11, 2019

GU272 Referendum

Students vote in favor of a referendum to establish a semesterly fee that would fund benefits for descendants of the 272 people sold by Georgetown in 1838. An overwhelming 66.1 percent of students vote "yes" for an increase of $27.20 each semester that would go towards this fund.

Learn More

Georgetown's Response

Georgetown announces that they will launch an initiative supporting community-based projects with GU272 descendants. President DeGioia claims that the funding will be comparable to or higher than the amount that would have been raised by the GU272 fee proposed by students.

Learn More
March 16, 2021

Descendants Truth and Reconciliation Foundation

The GU272 Descendants Association partners with the Society of Jesus to create a foundation with the goal of investing heavily in dismantling the remnants of slavery and mitigating racism. The Foundation is comitted to investing in the educational aspirations of descendants and future generations and in pursuing truth, reconciliation and transformation in the human family.
